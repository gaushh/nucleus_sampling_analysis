Thresholding as a Decoding Strategy for LLMs

Introduction: Decoding strategies such as Nucleus Sampling and Top-K Sampling have been instrumental in the success of LLMs.

Is Nucleus Sampling the best way to decode? We analyze the generated text from Nucleus Sampling to find some of its shortcomings and propose a new sampling strategy hoping to solve these problems.

We also experiment with a dynamic thresholding strategy based on perplexity.

Weaknesses of Nucleus Sampling: The sampling method can miss out words with significant probability in fat-tailed distributions. This can impact the creativity of the model. Nucleus sampling can end up picking words with very low probabilities in thin-tailed distributions, leading to incoherent text.

Our Approach: Thresholding for decoding

We evaluate a threshold-based decoding strategy. The key idea is to use a threshold on the probability values of the tokens and sample from only tokens that have probabilities higher than the thresholding probability. The value of this can be a hyperparameter as well as adjusted based on perplexity as we discuss later.

How thresholding solves the previous shortcomings: Thresholding avoids the problems of top-k as the dynamically changing candidate pool is still obtained. Thresholding also addresses the problems of nucleus sampling by being robust to flat and skewed distributions that might cause issues.
